Scope, resources & time

Last updated: Jun 24, 2026 · 4 min

Every project gives you three levers, and you rarely control all of them. Knowing which one you can move — and saying so out loud — is half of delivery management.

The three levers

When a deadline and a scope and a team collide, you're really negotiating across three things:

  • Scope — what the project will actually deliver.
  • Resources — how many engineers you assign.
  • Time — how long you have.

Sometimes you can flex all three; usually you can't. Everything is a trade-off — name the one that's movable.

Pro Tip: When someone asks for more scope and an earlier date, don't say "no" — say "which lever moves?" It reframes a conflict as a shared decision.

Making the trade visible

The mistake new managers make is absorbing the tension silently. Put the levers on the table so the trade-off is a decision your stakeholders make with you, not a surprise you eat alone.
